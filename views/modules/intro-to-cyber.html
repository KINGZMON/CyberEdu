Cybersecurity 101: Keepin' Your Digital World Safe

Get the lowdown on cybersecurity basics and learn how to stay safe online.

Understanding Cybersecurity

What is Cybersecurity?

  • Cybersecurity 101: Think of cybersecurity like a digital bodyguard. It's all about protecting your devices and data from online baddies who want to mess with your stuff.
  • Why it Matters: In today's digital age, nearly everything we do is online. From social media to online banking, cybersecurity protects our personal information and ensures our digital activities are secure.

Common Threats:

  • Phishing: Imagine getting a DM from someone pretending to be your fave celeb, asking for your password. That's phishing—tricking you into giving up your info.
  • Malware: This is nasty software that can wreck your computer or phone. It's like catching a virus IRL, but for your tech.
  • Identity Theft: Picture someone stealing your identity and buying stuff with your money. Scary, right? That's why keeping your info safe is a big deal.

Basic Online Safety Practices

Creating Strong Passwords:

  • Mix It Up: Use a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Think "P@ssw0rd!" instead of "password123".
  • Avoid the Obvious: No pet names or birthdays—those are too easy to guess.
  • Longer is Better: Go for at least 8 characters. The longer, the better!

Recognizing Safe Websites:

  • Check for HTTPS: Only trust sites that start with "https://". The "s" stands for secure, like a secret handshake.
  • Look for the Lock: A padlock icon in the address bar means the site is secure. No padlock? No deal!

Avoiding Suspicious Links and Emails:

  • Be Skeptical: If you get an unexpected email or message, especially asking for your info, think twice. If it smells fishy, it probably is.
  • Hover Before You Click: Hover over links to see where they lead before clicking. If it looks weird, don’t click!
